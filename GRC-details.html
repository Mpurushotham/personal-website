NIST CSF 2.0

NIST CSF 2.0 is a cybersecurity risk management framework consisting of 6 core functions.

Govern

Defines strategy, roles, and governance structure.

Identify

Asset inventory, supply chain risks, threat modeling.

Protect

Identity management, access control, encryption.

Detect

Monitoring anomalies, SIEM, threat hunting.

Respond

Incident response, containment, forensic analysis.

Recover

Business continuity, disaster recovery, improvements.

SOC 2

SOC 2 evaluates controls across 5 Trust Services Criteria (TSC).

Security (Required)

Access controls, firewalls, monitoring.

Availability

Redundancy, uptime, continuity.

Confidentiality

Encryption, restricted access.

Privacy

Personal data handling and retention.

Processing Integrity

Accurate, complete, authorized processing.

ISO 27001

Information Security Management System (ISMS) standard with 93 Annex A controls.

PCI DSS

Security standard for protecting cardholder data, includes 12 high-level requirements.

HIPAA

Healthcare data privacy and security requirements including the Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules.

GDPR

EU data protection law with rights for individuals and obligations for organizations.

Risk Management

Includes risk identification, assessment, scoring, treatment planning, and continuous monitoring.

Policies & Procedures

Includes Security Policies, Access Control Policy, Incident Response Plan, Business Continuity Plan, and more.

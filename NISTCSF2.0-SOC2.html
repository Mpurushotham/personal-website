NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 — Full Detailed Breakdown

Overview of NIST CSF 2.0

NIST CSF 2.0 (released 2024) updates the original CSF 1.1 and provides a more modern, flexible, and enterprise-friendly cybersecurity risk management framework. It is designed for organizations of any size.

Major updates include:

  • New Govern Function
  • Greater emphasis on supply chain cybersecurity
  • Profiles, tiers, and outcomes refined
  • Better alignment with privacy, AI, and resilience needs
The 6 Core Functions of NIST CSF 2.0

NIST CSF 2.0 organizes cybersecurity into 6 major functions:

1. GOVERN

  • Establish organizational risk management strategy
  • Define cybersecurity supply chain risk requirements
  • Set roles, responsibilities, and oversight models
  • Develop policies and enterprise governance structures

2. IDENTIFY

  • Asset inventory (IT, OT, cloud)
  • Risk assessment & business environment analysis
  • Understanding threats, vulnerabilities, and impact

3. PROTECT

  • Identity & access management
  • Data security and encryption
  • Awareness training
  • Endpoint & network protections

4. DETECT

  • Threat monitoring & anomaly detection
  • Security event logging & SIEM integrations
  • Continuous monitoring programs

5. RESPOND

  • Incident response plans
  • Threat containment
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Forensic analysis

6. RECOVER

  • System restoration after incidents
  • Resilience improvements
  • Business continuity processes

These functions define a complete life cycle of cybersecurity readiness.

NIST CSF 2.0 Profiles & Tiers

CSF Profiles

A Profile describes the current and target cybersecurity posture. Organizations use Profiles to conduct gap analysis and roadmap improvements.

Maturity Tiers

Tiers describe risk awareness and integration:

  • Tier 1: Partial
  • Tier 2: Risk Informed
  • Tier 3: Repeatable
  • Tier 4: Adaptive

SOC 2 (System and Organization Controls) — Full Detailed Breakdown

Overview of SOC 2

SOC 2 is an auditing framework created by AICPA to evaluate the controls of service providers that store or process customer data.

It applies heavily to SaaS, cloud providers, MSPs, financial services, and technology companies.

Trust Services Criteria (TSC)

SOC 2 evaluates controls across 5 Trust Services Criteria (TSC):

  • Security (Required): Firewalls, access controls, intrusion detection.
  • Availability: Uptime, redundancy, DDoS protection.
  • Confidentiality: Encryption, data access restrictions.
  • Processing Integrity: Accurate and authorized data processing.
  • Privacy: Handling of personal information according to policies.
SOC 2 Type I vs Type II

Type I

Evaluates whether controls are properly designed at a specific point in time.

Type II

Evaluates design AND operating effectiveness over 3–12 months.

Type II is the gold standard for customer assurance.

SOC 2 Report Components
  • Management assertion
  • Independent auditor’s opinion
  • System description
  • Controls tested and results
  • Optional security metrics and risk analyses
